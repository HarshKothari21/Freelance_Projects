US Commericial Flights¶

by Abeer Alahmadi¶

Preliminary Wrangling¶

The Flight data contain ~607K records from the year 2020 taken from the United States Department of Transportation.

  • Year - Year of Data record, Quarter - Contains values from 1-4, Day-of-week - 1(Monday) to 7(Sunday)
  • DepTime - actual departure time (local, hhmm) and ArrTime - actual arrival time (local, hhmm)
  • Distance - Distance between airports (miles)
  • Dest,Origin - Destination Airport and Origin Airport
  • DestCityName, OriginCityName - Destination Airport and Origin Airport, City Name
  • Cancelled - was the flight cancelled?
  • CancellationCode - reason for cancellation (A = carrier, B = weather, C = NAS, D = security)
  • CarrierDelay - in minutes
  • LateAircraftDelay - in minutes
  • SecurityDelay - in minutes
  • NASDelay - in minutes
  • WeatherDelay - in minutes
  • ArrDelay - Difference in minutes between scheduled and actual arrival time. Early arrivals show negative numbers.
  • DepDelay - Difference in minutes between scheduled and actual departure time. Early departures show negative numbers.

What is the structure of your dataset?¶

Dataset contains around 607k records and 36 features.

  • Some of these null values are self explanatory. Like All the delays including Securtiy, weather or Carrier delays have same number of null values indicating 0 Delay during the particular flight.
  • Thus we can say that from 607K records, 525K records had No Delay.
  • Similarly, There are 600K null values in Cancellation_code indicating there could be only 7000 flights got cancelled among 607K flights.

What is/are the main feature(s) of interest in your dataset?¶

Main Features in dataset are Delays(ARR_DELAY, DEPT_DELAY) and reason behind the delays(WEATHER, CARRIER, SECURITY,etc), , Flight cancelled and reason of cancellation

I created this feature to know which section of time can have maximum delays and thus in order to related with other featured under bivariable exploration more accurately.
